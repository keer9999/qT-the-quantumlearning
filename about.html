About the Project

qT – Quantum Technology Learning Platform is an academic educational initiative developed in the year 2025 as part of a Community Service Project (CSP) at JNTUACEA (Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Anantapur College of Engineering, Anantapuramu), under the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE).

The platform bridges the gap between traditional engineering education and the rapidly emerging field of quantum technologies, offering a structured, interactive pathway from physical fundamentals to advanced quantum algorithms and cryptography.

Developed: 2025 JNTUACEA, Anantapuramu ECE Department CSP

What is qT?

qT stands for Quantum Technology – a student-built, open-access learning platform designed to take learners from zero knowledge all the way to understanding quantum algorithms and quantum cryptographic protocols.

Every module on the platform uses interactive simulators, LaTeX mathematics, 3D visualisations, and step-by-step explanations – all freely accessible through a browser.

Objective

Quantum technologies are transforming computation, communication and security. Yet students often struggle due to abstract mathematics and the absence of structured resources.

qT was built to:

  • Introduce quantum concepts in an intuitive manner
  • Provide step-by-step learning from fundamentals to advanced topics
  • Bridge physical intuition with mathematical rigour
  • Support students from all academic backgrounds

Platform Capabilities

Physics Foundations

Wave-particle duality, superposition principle, uncertainty principle and quantum mechanics fundamentals through interactive visual modules.

physics/physics-foundations.html

Qubits & Mathematics

Dirac notation, linear algebra, state vectors and the variational method — the mathematical language of quantum mechanics, rendered in LaTeX.

math/math-foundations.html

Superposition & Measurement

Interactive modules covering quantum superposition, measurement collapse, probability & shots, and classical vs qubit comparison.

superposition.html · measurement.html

3D Bloch Sphere Simulator

A real-time 3D Bloch sphere simulator for visualising qubit states, gate rotations, noise effects, and quantum phase — built with Three.js.

quantum-phase-advanced/index.html

Quantum Gates Library

Comprehensive library of single-qubit and multi-qubit gates (Pauli, Hadamard, Phase, CNOT, CZ, SWAP, Rotation) with LaTeX matrix representations.

quantum-gates.html

Interactive Gate Composer

An advanced visual gate composer for building quantum circuits step-by-step, with real-time Bloch sphere feedback and animation.

single-qubit-gates/gates_advanced.html

Quantum Algorithms

Step-by-step, LaTeX-rendered explanations of Deutsch-Jozsa, Grover, Shor, Bernstein-Vazirani, Simon's, and QFT algorithms with circuit diagrams.

quantum-algorithms.html

Quantum Cryptography

Modules on BB84, E91, quantum key distribution, post-quantum cryptography and the security implications of quantum computing for classical encryption.

qt-learning-platform.html · crypto section

Noise & Imperfections

Dedicated module on quantum decoherence, error types, and why noisy quantum hardware matters — a vital topic for NISQ-era understanding.

noise-imperfections/noise.html

Advanced & External Courses

Curated links to IBM Quantum Learning, qBraid, and NPTEL courses — helping students progress from platform foundations to industry-grade quantum computing.

qt-learning-platform.html · advanced section

Quantum Phase & Hamiltonians

Interactive exploration of quantum phase, Bloch sphere rotations, Hamiltonians, and quantum phase estimation—visualised in real time.

quantum-phase.html

Variational Method Simulator

Connects quantum mechanics with optimisation algorithms — key for understanding variational quantum eigensolvers (VQE) and near-term quantum computing.

variational.html · variationalMethod.html
