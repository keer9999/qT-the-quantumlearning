Noise & Imperfections in Quantum Computers

Why Are Quantum Computers Noisy?

Quantum computers use qubits, which exist in delicate quantum states. These states are extremely sensitive to their surroundings. Any unwanted interaction with the environment introduces noise.

Simple analogy:
Talking loudly near someone who is trying to concentrate makes them lose focus. Similarly, environmental disturbances make qubits lose their quantum information.

Sources of Noise & Imperfections

  • Thermal vibrations
  • Electromagnetic interference
  • Imperfect control pulses
  • Material defects
  • Cross-talk between qubits

How Do We Reduce Noise?

  • Quantum error correction
  • Better cryogenic isolation
  • Improved materials
  • Fault-tolerant architectures

Currently, trapped-ion qubits have lower noise, while superconducting qubits scale faster.
