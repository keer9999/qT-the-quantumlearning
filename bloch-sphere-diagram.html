Bloch Sphere — Labeled Diagram

All components, axes, angles, states and projection lines

θ — Theta (Polar Angle)
Angle from Z-axis to state vector. Controls the probability amplitude ratio of |0⟩ vs |1⟩.
Range: 0 ≤ θ ≤ π  (0° to 180°)
θ=0 → |0⟩  |  θ=π → |1⟩  |  θ=π/2 → equator
φ — Phi (Azimuthal Angle)
Angle around Z-axis in the X-Y plane. Sets the relative quantum phase between basis states.
Range: 0 ≤ φ < 2π  (0° to 360°)
φ=0 → |+⟩  |  φ=π/2 → |+i⟩  |  φ=π → |−⟩
|ψ⟩ — Quantum State Vector
A unit vector on the sphere surface. Every pure single-qubit state maps uniquely here.
|ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ) sin(θ/2)|1⟩
Z-Axis — Computational Basis
Measurement axis. North = |0⟩, South = |1⟩. Aligned with Pauli-Z (σz) operator.
P(0) = cos²(θ/2)  |  P(1) = sin²(θ/2)
X-Axis — Hadamard Basis
Eigenstates of Pauli-X. H gate rotates Z↔X. States |+⟩ and |−⟩ live here.
|+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2 → θ=π/2, φ=0
|−⟩ = (|0⟩−|1⟩)/√2 → θ=π/2, φ=π
Y-Axis — Phase Basis
Eigenstates of Pauli-Y. The imaginary phase states |+i⟩ and |−i⟩ reside here.
|+i⟩ = (|0⟩+i|1⟩)/√2 → θ=π/2, φ=π/2
|−i⟩ = (|0⟩−i|1⟩)/√2 → θ=π/2, φ=3π/2

General Qubit State Equation

|ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2) |0⟩ + eiφ · sin(θ/2) |1⟩
θ ∈ [0, π] — polar angle (amplitude ratio)  |  φ ∈ [0, 2π) — azimuthal angle (relative phase)

Cartesian Coordinates of the Bloch Vector

How θ and φ map to X, Y, Z components of the state vector r̂ = (x, y, z)

X Component
x = sinθ cosφ
|+⟩ at x=+1  |  |−⟩ at x=−1
Y Component
y = sinθ sinφ
|+i⟩ at y=+1  |  |−i⟩ at y=−1
Z Component
z = cosθ
|0⟩ at z=+1  |  |1⟩ at z=−1

Quantum Gates as Bloch Sphere Rotations

Every single-qubit unitary $U \in SU(2)$ corresponds to a rotation $R \in SO(3)$ of the Bloch sphere

X
Pauli-X  /  Quantum NOT
$R_x(\pi)$ — $\pi$ rotation around $\hat{x}$-axis
$$X = \begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}$$
$|0\rangle \leftrightarrow |1\rangle$. Swaps north and south poles. Bloch vector: $(x,y,z)\to(x,-y,-z)$.
Y
Pauli-Y
$R_y(\pi)$ — $\pi$ rotation around $\hat{y}$-axis
$$Y = \begin{pmatrix}0 & -i \\ i & 0\end{pmatrix}$$
$|0\rangle \to i|1\rangle,\;|1\rangle \to -i|0\rangle$. Bloch vector: $(x,y,z)\to(-x,y,-z)$.
Z
Pauli-Z  /  Phase Flip
$R_z(\pi)$ — $\pi$ rotation around $\hat{z}$-axis
$$Z = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{pmatrix}$$
$|{+}\rangle \leftrightarrow |{-}\rangle$. Leaves $|0\rangle,|1\rangle$ invariant. Bloch vector: $(x,y,z)\to(-x,-y,z)$.
H
Hadamard
$\pi$ rotation around $\frac{\hat{x}+\hat{z}}{\sqrt{2}}$
$$H = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1\end{pmatrix}$$
Swaps $X \leftrightarrow Z$ axes. $|0\rangle \to |{+}\rangle,\;|1\rangle \to |{-}\rangle$. Bloch vector: $(x,y,z)\to(z,-y,x)$.
S
Phase Gate   $S = T^2$
$R_z\!\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)$ — $90°$ Z-rotation
$$S = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & i\end{pmatrix}$$
$|1\rangle \to i|1\rangle$. Maps $|{+}\rangle \to |{+i}\rangle$. Key Clifford group gate. $\varphi \to \varphi + \tfrac{\pi}{2}$.
T
$\pi/8$ Gate
$R_z\!\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)$ — $45°$ Z-rotation
$$T = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\pi/4}\end{pmatrix}$$
$|1\rangle \to e^{i\pi/4}|1\rangle$. Essential for universal quantum computation (with $H + \text{CNOT}$). $\varphi \to \varphi + \tfrac{\pi}{4}$.
General Single-Qubit Rotation
$$R_{\hat{n}}(\theta) = \cos\frac{\theta}{2}\,I - i\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\,(\hat{n}\cdot\vec{\sigma})$$
$\hat{n}=(n_x,n_y,n_z)$ — rotation axis  |  $\theta$ — rotation angle  |  $\vec{\sigma}=(\sigma_x,\sigma_y,\sigma_z)$ — Pauli vector
