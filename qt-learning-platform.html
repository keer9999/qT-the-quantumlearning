Quantum Cryptography

BB84 Quantum Key Distribution

BB84 Protocol is the first and most famous quantum key distribution (QKD) protocol, invented by Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard in 1984. It allows two parties (Alice and Bob) to securely share encryption keys that are guaranteed to be secure by the laws of quantum mechanics.

Why It Matters: Unlike classical encryption methods (like RSA) that can be broken by powerful computers or quantum computers using Shor's algorithm, BB84 provides information-theoretic security. Any eavesdropping attempt is automatically detected!

How It Works: Alice sends Bob photons with random polarizations (â†‘, â†’, â†—, â†–). Bob measures them with randomly chosen polarizing filters. After transmission, they publicly compare which filters they used (notthe results!) and keep only the bits where they used matching filters. If an eavesdropper (Eve) intercepts the photons, the error rate increases, revealing her presence.

Real-World Applications:

  • Banking & Financial Institutions
  • Government Secure Communications
  • Healthcare Data Protection
  • Future Quantum Internet Infrastructure

Other QKD Protocols

E91 Protocol: Uses quantum entanglement instead of individual photons. Security is guaranteed by Bell's inequality violations.

B92 Protocol: A simplified version of BB84 using only two non-orthogonal states.

All quantum cryptography protocols share one key advantage: security guaranteed by quantum physics, not computational complexity.
